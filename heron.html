Heron

The Heron is a striking, large wading bird known for its long legs, slender neck, and sharp beak, which it uses with precision to catch fish and other aquatic prey. Often seen standing still in shallow water or gliding gracefully through the air with slow, deliberate wingbeats, the Heron embodies both elegance and patience. Its muted grey, blue, or white plumage allows it to blend seamlessly into its wetland surroundings, making it a master of stealth in its natural habitat.
